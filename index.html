The Life of a Genius

Early Life

Nikola Tesla was born on July 10, 1856, in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia). From an early age, Tesla displayed extraordinary mental abilities and an eidetic memory. His father, Milutin Tesla, was a Serbian Orthodox priest, and his mother, Đuka Tesla, was an inventor in her own right, creating household appliances.

Education & Early Career

Tesla studied electrical engineering at the Austrian Polytechnic in Graz and later at the University of Prague. In 1881, he moved to Budapest to work for the Central Telephone Exchange, where he made his first major invention - a telephone repeater.

The American Dream

In 1884, Tesla immigrated to the United States with only 4 cents in his pocket and a letter of recommendation to Thomas Edison. He worked briefly for Edison but left due to disagreements over AC vs DC current systems.

Later Years

Despite his brilliant inventions, Tesla struggled financially in his later years. He died alone in room 3327 of the New Yorker Hotel on January 7, 1943, at the age of 86. His ashes are now displayed in a golden sphere at the Nikola Tesla Museum in Belgrade.

Revolutionary Inventions

Alternating Current (AC)

Tesla's AC system revolutionized power distribution, making it possible to transmit electricity over long distances efficiently. This system powers the modern world.

Year: 1888
Impact: Powers 90% of the world's electricity
Patent: US 381,968

Tesla Coil

A high-voltage, high-frequency transformer that produces spectacular electrical discharges. The foundation for radio technology and wireless communication.

Year: 1891
Impact: Radio, TV, wireless technology
Patent: US 454,622

Wireless Energy Transmission

Tesla envisioned a world where electricity could be transmitted wirelessly through the air. His Wardenclyffe Tower was designed to demonstrate this technology.

Year: 1901
Impact: Modern wireless charging
Status: Experimental

Radio Technology

Tesla developed the fundamental principles of radio communication, including the Tesla oscillator and wireless transmission of signals.

Year: 1893
Impact: Modern radio, TV, WiFi
Patent: US 645,576

Induction Motor

The first practical AC motor, which converts electrical energy into mechanical energy. Still used in most household appliances today.

Year: 1887
Impact: Industrial motors, appliances
Patent: US 381,968

X-Ray Technology

Tesla experimented with X-rays before Wilhelm Röntgen's official discovery, creating some of the first X-ray images of the human body.

Year: 1894
Impact: Medical imaging
Status: Experimental
